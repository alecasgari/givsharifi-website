FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about neurosurgery consultations and treatment.

Where does Prof. Giv Sharifi practice?

Prof. Sharifi sees patients in Dubai (City Walk, UAE) and Tehran (Tavanir, Iran). Online consultations are available for international patients planning travel or a second opinion.

What conditions does he treat?

Brain tumors, pituitary adenomas, herniated discs, spinal stenosis, hydrocephalus, spinal cord tumors, and other complex brain and spine conditions requiring surgical or minimally invasive treatment.

Is the first consultation free?

Yes. You can book a free consultation to discuss your symptoms, imaging, and treatment options — in person in Dubai or Tehran, or remotely.

Do you offer medical tourism for surgery in Iran?

Yes. International patients can arrange brain or spinal surgery in Tehran with pre-travel consultation, hospital coordination, accommodation guidance, and English-speaking support.

What is minimally invasive neurosurgery?

Techniques such as endoscopic pituitary surgery and minimally invasive spine surgery (MISS) use smaller incisions and advanced imaging to reduce recovery time while maintaining surgical precision.

How do I send my MRI or CT scans?

Contact us via the consultation form or WhatsApp. Our team will guide you on securely sharing imaging before your appointment.
