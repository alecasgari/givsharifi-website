Types of Surgical Procedures for Brain Tumours

The surgical strategy for a brain tumour depends largely on the location, size, and type of the growth identified during your initial assessment. Craniotomy is the most common procedure, where a section of the skull is temporarily removed to provide the surgeon with direct access to the tumour site. Prof. Giv Sharifi employs advanced neuronavigation systems that act like a GPS for the brain, allowing for extreme precision during the removal process. These tools help ensure that the surgeon can navigate around critical structures, significantly reducing the risk of complications and enhancing the overall safety of the operation for every patient.

In addition to traditional craniotomy, there are minimally invasive techniques that may be suitable for certain types of brain cancer treatment. Endoscopic surgery, for instance, involves using small incisions and specialized cameras to reach tumours located deep within the skull or near the base of the brain. These approaches often result in shorter hospital stays, less postoperative pain, and faster recovery times compared to open surgery. During your consultation, your surgeon will assess your specific anatomy and tumour characteristics to determine if you are a candidate for these less invasive methods, ensuring your treatment plan is tailored to your needs.

The Diagnostic Process and Pre-Surgical Planning

Before any surgical intervention takes place, a thorough diagnostic process is required to map out the tumour and its relationship to essential brain pathways. Patients typically undergo high-resolution MRI or CT scans, which provide detailed images that guide the surgical team. Prof. Giv Sharifi uses these images to perform detailed preoperative planning, often utilizing functional MRI to identify areas of the brain responsible for speech, movement, and vision. This meticulous preparation is vital, as it allows the surgical team to anticipate challenges and develop a strategy that maximizes tumour removal while protecting your cognitive and physical abilities.

The planning phase also involves a detailed discussion about the potential risks and benefits associated with the procedure. Your neurosurgeon will explain the expected outcomes and the steps taken to mitigate risks during the operation. This transparent communication is essential for building trust and ensuring you feel informed and supported throughout your journey. By focusing on evidence-based practices and patient-centered care, the team ensures that every detail is accounted for, from the initial imaging sessions to the final surgical plan, providing a clear roadmap for your upcoming treatment and recovery process.

What to Expect During Recovery and Rehabilitation

Recovery after brain tumour surgery is a gradual process that requires patience and dedicated support from a medical team. Immediately following the procedure, patients are monitored closely in the intensive care unit to ensure neurological stability and manage any postoperative symptoms. Prof. Giv Sharifi and his team prioritize pain management and early mobilization to help patients regain their strength as quickly as possible. It is normal to experience some fatigue or mild discomfort during the initial days, but these symptoms are managed with appropriate medication and supportive care to ensure you remain comfortable throughout your stay.

Long-term rehabilitation is often a critical component of the recovery journey for many patients. Depending on the location of the tumour and the impact of the surgery, you may benefit from physical, occupational, or speech therapy to regain full function. These specialized programs are designed to help you adapt and improve your daily activities, fostering independence and confidence. Your surgeon will coordinate with a team of rehabilitation experts to create a personalized plan that addresses your specific needs, ensuring that you have the necessary resources to achieve the best possible quality of life following your treatment.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    How long does brain tumour surgery typically take?

    The duration of brain tumour surgery varies significantly depending on the complexity of the tumour, its size, and its location within the brain. While some procedures may take only a few hours, more complex cases that require intricate mapping and careful navigation around critical structures can take longer. Prof. Giv Sharifi will provide you with a more accurate estimate based on your specific diagnostic imaging and surgical plan. The primary focus is always on achieving the best surgical outcome rather than the speed of the procedure, ensuring your safety throughout the entire operation.

    What are the common symptoms of a brain tumour?

    Brain tumour symptoms can vary widely depending on where the growth is located and how much it affects surrounding brain tissue. Common indicators include persistent headaches that may worsen in the morning, unexplained nausea or vomiting, vision changes, and seizures. Some patients may also experience difficulty with balance, speech, or cognitive functions. If you notice any persistent or concerning neurological symptoms, it is vital to seek a professional evaluation immediately. Early detection through diagnostic imaging is the most effective way to identify potential issues and begin appropriate treatment planning with a qualified neurosurgeon.

    Will I need radiation or chemotherapy after surgery?

    Whether or not you require additional treatments like radiation or chemotherapy depends on the pathology of the tumour, which is determined after the surgery through a biopsy. Some benign tumours may be fully treated with surgery alone, while malignant tumours often require a combination of therapies to ensure the best long-term results. Prof. Giv Sharifi works closely with a team of oncologists to determine if adjuvant therapy is necessary for your specific case. Your surgeon will discuss these possibilities with you during your postoperative consultations, ensuring that your long-term care plan is comprehensive and evidence-based.

    How long is the recovery period after a craniotomy?

    Recovery is a highly individual process that depends on the extent of the surgery and your overall health status. Most patients spend a few days in the hospital for initial monitoring before transitioning to home recovery. While you may be able to return to light daily activities within a few weeks, a full recovery and return to work or more strenuous exercise often take several months. Your neurosurgeon will provide a structured recovery timeline and guide you through each phase, ensuring that you regain your strength and function safely and effectively under professional supervision.

    Is it possible to have a brain tumour removed safely?

    Modern neurosurgery has made significant advancements in removing brain tumours safely, even those located in challenging areas. By utilizing high-tech imaging, neuronavigation, and minimally invasive techniques, surgeons can achieve high rates of success while preserving neurological function. Prof. Giv Sharifi uses these advanced tools to minimize risks and maximize the removal of the tumour. While no surgery is without risks, the combination of expert surgical skill and state-of-the-art technology provides the safest possible environment for your procedure, significantly improving the chances of a positive outcome for your health and future recovery.
