FAQ

Physiotherapy — Frequently Asked Questions

What is neurological physiotherapy?

It is specialised rehabilitation for the nervous system — helping patients recover movement, balance, and function after brain surgery, spinal surgery, stroke, or nerve conditions. It differs from sports or orthopaedic physiotherapy by focusing on neurological recovery patterns.

When should I start physiotherapy after neurosurgery?

Early mobilisation often begins in hospital. Outpatient neurological physiotherapy typically starts once your surgeon clears you — usually within 2–6 weeks depending on the procedure. Prof. Sharifi's team will advise on the right timing for your case.

Is physiotherapy available in Dubai?

Yes. Neurological physiotherapy programmes are offered at City Walk, Dubai, integrated with Prof. Sharifi's neurosurgical practice. Patients who had surgery in Tehran can also receive rehab guidance and follow-up planning.

How many sessions will I need?

Session count varies widely — from a few weeks for straightforward spinal recovery to several months for complex brain or stroke rehabilitation. Your programme is adjusted at each review based on progress.

Can physiotherapy help with balance and walking problems?

Yes. Gait and balance training are core components of neuro rehabilitation. Progressive exercises, vestibular work, and functional practice reduce fall risk and improve walking confidence.

How do I book a physiotherapy assessment?

Contact us via the consultation form or WhatsApp. Mention your surgery date and current symptoms — we will arrange an initial assessment and discuss programme options.
