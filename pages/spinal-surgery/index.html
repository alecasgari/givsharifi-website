Procedures

Spinal Surgery Procedures Offered

Evidence-based techniques tailored to your diagnosis, anatomy, and lifestyle goals.

Microdiscectomy

Removal of herniated disc material compressing a nerve root — gold standard for lumbar disc surgery with high success rates.

Laminectomy

Removal of lamina bone to decompress the spinal canal — effective for stenosis causing neurogenic claudication.

Spinal Fusion

Instrumentation and bone graft to stabilise unstable segments — for spondylolisthesis and deformity.

Minimally Invasive Spine Surgery

MISS via tubular retractors — smaller scars, less muscle disruption, and often shorter hospital stays.

Cervical Decompression

Anterior or posterior approaches for disc disease and myelopathy — preserving cord function.

Foraminotomy

Enlargement of the nerve exit foramen — relieves foraminal stenosis and radiating arm or leg pain.

Spinal Surgery — Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the best spine surgeon in Dubai?

Prof. Giv Sharifi is a DHA-licensed neurosurgeon with 25+ years treating herniated discs, stenosis, and complex spine conditions. He practices at City Walk, Dubai, and Tavanir, Tehran — with free consultations for local and international patients.

When is surgery needed for a herniated disc?

Surgery is considered when pain persists despite conservative treatment, or when there is progressive weakness, cauda equina syndrome, or significant neurological deficit. Microdiscectomy is often effective for lumbar disc herniation with sciatica.

What is the recovery time after spinal surgery?

Recovery depends on the procedure. Minimally invasive microdiscectomy may allow return to desk work within 2–4 weeks; fusion surgery typically requires longer rehabilitation. Your personalised timeline is discussed at consultation.

What is minimally invasive spine surgery (MISS)?

MISS uses smaller incisions and tubular retractors to access the spine with less muscle damage than open surgery. It is suitable for many disc and stenosis cases — Prof. Sharifi will advise if you are a candidate.

Can spinal stenosis be treated without fusion?

Many stenosis cases are treated with decompression alone (laminectomy). Fusion is added when there is instability, spondylolisthesis, or deformity requiring instrumentation.
