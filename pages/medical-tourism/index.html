FAQ

Medical Tourism — Frequently Asked Questions

What does door-to-door medical tourism include?

Everything from your first enquiry at home: consultation, visa support, flight and hotel coordination, airport transfers, hospital booking, surgery, recovery stay, return travel, and remote follow-up. You are never left to figure out Tehran alone.

How do I get a medical visa for Iran?

After your consultation is approved, we provide a medical invitation letter and guide you through the visa application for your nationality. Requirements vary — our team sends a personalised checklist.

How much does brain or spinal surgery cost in Iran?

Costs are typically a fraction of UAE or European prices. The exact quote depends on your diagnosis, procedure, and hospital stay length. Send your MRI for a personalised estimate — no obligation.

Can a family member travel with me?

Yes. We help arrange companion accommodation and keep your family informed throughout surgery and recovery. Many patients travel with a spouse or relative.

Do you speak Arabic?

Yes. Our coordination team supports English and Arabic. Watch our Arabic patient video above — السياحة العلاجية في إيران.

How long do I need to stay in Tehran?

Typically 10–21 days depending on procedure — pre-op tests, surgery, and initial recovery. We plan your itinerary before you book flights so there are no surprises.
