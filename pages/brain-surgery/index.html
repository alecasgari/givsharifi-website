Techniques

Advanced Brain Surgery Techniques

Technology-guided surgery to maximize tumor removal while protecting critical brain function.

Neuronavigation

Real-time 3D guidance during craniotomy — precise localization of tumors relative to eloquent brain areas.

Intraoperative MRI

Imaging during surgery to confirm extent of resection before closing — especially valuable for glioma surgery.

Awake Brain Surgery

Patient remains conscious for speech and motor mapping — essential when tumors lie near language or movement centres.

Minimally Invasive Cranial

Smaller bone openings and keyhole approaches where anatomy allows — less trauma, shorter recovery.

Endoscopic Assistance

Endoscopic visualization for deep or ventricular lesions — complementary to traditional microsurgical technique.

Functional Preservation

Every plan prioritizes speech, cognition, vision, and mobility — balancing oncological goals with quality of life.

What types of brain tumors does Prof. Sharifi treat?

Gliomas, meningiomas, metastatic brain tumors, skull base lesions, and related cranial conditions. Pituitary tumors may be treated via endoscopic transsphenoidal surgery when appropriate.

How long is recovery after brain tumor surgery?

Recovery varies by tumor type, location, and surgical approach. Minimally invasive cases may allow discharge within days; complex procedures require longer hospital and rehabilitation periods. Your personalised timeline is discussed at consultation.

Is minimally invasive brain surgery available?

Yes — when clinically appropriate. Keyhole craniotomy, neuronavigation, and endoscopic assistance can reduce surgical trauma. Not every tumor is suitable; Prof. Sharifi will advise based on your imaging.

Can I get a second opinion before brain surgery?

Absolutely. Book a free consultation and share your MRI/CT scans via our secure process. Many international patients seek a second opinion before committing to surgery at home or abroad.
